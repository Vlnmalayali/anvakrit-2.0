About NFSU

The National Forensic Sciences University, an Institution of National Importance under the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, is the world's first and only University dedicated to forensic sciences and allied disciplines. Established by the Government of India Act, 2020, NFSU encompasses forensics, engineering, technology, life sciences, behavioral science, clinical and forensic psychology, law, pharmacy, cyber security, digital forensics, management, forensic accounting and interdisciplinary areas.

About The Event

Anvakrit 2.0: Beyond the Crime Scene

After the resounding success of its first edition, Anvakrit 2.0 returns from 11th-13th February 2026 with greater depth, tougher challenges, and a bold new vision. Centered around the theme of Digital Forensics, the competition invites participants to step Beyond the Crime Scene and test their investigative acumen in dynamic, real-world scenarios.

"In the world of forensics, complexity is not the exception, it is the rule."

A crime scene is never simple. It can unfold across multiple locations, conceal digital trails alongside physical traces, and demand a balance of scientific precision and human judgment. Anvakrit 2.0 is crafted to push participants beyond textbook learning, challenging them to think, act, and adapt like true professionals.

Competition Rounds

ROUND 1: Case File Investigation

The journey begins with a case file (online/offline) to test investigative, critical thinking and deductive skills. Teams must respond to questions within the given time limit.

ROUND 2: Poster Presentation of Findings

Online poster presentation showcasing case file findings and methods used. Most creative posters with accurate solutions advance to Round 3. Top teams based on cumulative scores of Rounds 1 & 2 qualify for the offline round.

ROUND 3: On-Campus Crime Scene

Top teams advance to offline round at NFSU Delhi Campus for:

  • Domain-specific sessions conducted by forensic experts
  • Lab visits of state-of-the-art facilities
  • Immersive crime scene investigation challenge integrating all domains
  • Focus on observation, teamwork, and practical application

Multi-layered, domain integrated immersive crime scene investigation with:

  • Criminological awareness and observation skills

  • Applied forensic skills under pressure
  • Prioritizing perishable evidence (footprints, dust, ice, smudged prints)

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common queries

What is Anvakrit 2.0?
Anvakrit 2.0 is a National Crime Scene Investigation Competition organized by NFSU. It's a three-day event (11th-13th February 2026) focused on Digital Forensics, offering participants hands-on experience in crime scene investigation, evidence collection, forensic analysis, and courtroom presentation.
Do I need prior forensic knowledge to participate?
For Category I (UG/Higher Secondary), no prior forensic knowledge is required. Curiosity, creativity, and teamwork are key. For Category II (PG/Professionals), basic understanding of forensic sciences or related fields is recommended.
Can I register individually?
Category I requires team registration (4 members, self-formed). Category II allows both individual registration (teams formed by organizers on-campus) and group registration (self-formed teams of 5 members).
What does the registration fee include?
The registration fee includes lunch, dinner, registration kits, expert workshops, lab visits, and certificate of participation. Accommodation (with breakfast) is optional at â‚¹500 per day.
Can I change my team after registration?
No. Once registration is completed, no changes in team names, composition, or roles will be permitted. This policy ensures fairness and proper event planning.
What happens if my team is eliminated?
For Category II, eliminated participants may choose to volunteer for the rest of the competition duration and will receive Guest Volunteer Certificates. This is a great opportunity to observe and learn from advanced investigations.
What materials will be provided?
The organizing committee will provide gloves, masks, evidence collection kits, stationery, lab equipment, and legal documents (if needed). Participants should bring their ID cards, personal stationery, and laptops if required.
Is accommodation mandatory?
No, accommodation is optional. If you choose to stay on campus, the charge is â‚¹500 per day, which includes breakfast. Accommodation details and partner hotel information will be provided post-registration.
What is the prize money?
Category I: Top 3 teams share a prize pool of INR 1.2 lakh. Category II: Top 2 teams share a prize pool of INR 1.2 lakh. All participants receive certificates of participation.
What are the surprise elements mentioned?
Throughout the competition, starting from registration, participants will encounter surprise puzzles, clues, and challenges that test awareness, vulnerability assessment, and knowledge of current crime trends. Keen observation from the start gives you an advantage!
